# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Indian families and temple pilgrims — mostly arriving on phones from Instagram/WhatsApp/search — planning a darshan trip to Vrindavan (Banke Bihari Ji). They are comparing a homestay against hotels and Airbnb, often deciding at night for a near-term weekend. A secondary audience is returning guests who already trust the host and just want dates + payment fast.

## Product Purpose

A direct-booking product for Pyari Kunj, a private 1BHK heritage homestay in old Vrindavan (650 m from Banke Bihari Temple). The page must do two jobs at once: make the guest feel the calm, cared-for character of the home, and let them complete a paid, verified reservation (Razorpay + server-verified, WhatsApp follow-up) in minutes. Success = paid direct bookings; the 10% direct discount is the incentive to skip Airbnb.

## Positioning

The temple side of Vrindavan, privately yours — booked direct with the host in two minutes, 10% cheaper than anywhere else.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: pick dates → Reserve & pay (Razorpay, server-verified).
- Secondary fallback: WhatsApp the host (FAB + confirmation flow); tertiary: view the Airbnb listing for external validation.
- The line a visitor remembers: "A quiet painted home, seven minutes' walk from Banke Bihari Ji."
- Belief ladder: (1) this is a real, beautiful, clean home — real photos; (2) the location genuinely works for darshan — distances; (3) other guests loved it — 14/14 five-star reviews, verifiable on Airbnb; (4) paying is safe — Razorpay, full refund if dates unavailable, server-side pricing; (5) I know what happens next — WhatsApp check-in details.
- Proof on hand: 21 real photos (img/), three review screenshots (img/review-1..3.webp), 5.0 rating across all six Airbnb categories, live availability from the Airbnb calendar.

## Brand Personality

Warm, unhurried, devoted. The house speaks like a gracious host, not a hotel: plain sentences, Hindi place-names kept intact, no sales pressure. Emotionally it should evoke the feeling of arriving somewhere already loved — marble underfoot, velvet cushions, a small mandir kept with care.

## Anti-references

- Airbnb's own listing template (nav, coral palette, category tabs, card grammar) — explicitly barred.
- The generic "heritage hotel" cream-and-saffron brochure with gold ornaments and Playfair/Fraunces serifs — the category's AI-default look.
- SaaS landing-page grammar: identical card grids, eyebrow labels over every section, hero metrics.
- Dark "luxury editorial" hotel sites where the property disappears behind typography.

## Design Principles

1. The house is the hero — photography carries emotion; UI stays quiet around it.
2. Booking is a product, not a widget — the money moment gets first-class clarity: price, dates, guests, total, and what happens after, without hunting.
3. Rooted, not themed — color and detail come from the actual rooms (marble, rani-pink velvet, pichwai panels, rosewood, brass), never from a generic "Indian heritage" kit.
4. Trust through specificity — real numbers (650 m, 14 reviews, ₹2,249), real names (Saar, Dinesh Ji), real policies, verifiable links.
5. Fast on a phone at night — one thumb, no surprise steps, no waiting on decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA contrast for all text; full keyboard operability of calendar, lightbox, FAQ and booking controls; visible focus; dialogs closable by Escape/backdrop/back; `prefers-reduced-motion` honored everywhere; touch targets ≥ 44px on mobile; no information conveyed by color alone.
